## Overview

**COPE (Counselling and Outreach for Peer Empowerment)** is a student-led initiative dedicated to improving the mental health of today's youngsters. It raises awareness about mental health issues and has a beneficial influence on young people.

## Vision

A stigma-free world.

## Mission

Raising awareness, promoting positive mental health, and enhancing the lives of students.

## Goals

- Increase awareness of mental health concerns
- De-stigmatise mental health and conversation
- Encourage asking for assistance
- Close the distance between students and mental health resources

## Key Mental Health Statistics

- 15–20% of people have anxiety disorders
- Suicide rate: 8.7%
- Academic stress levels: 38.4%

## Consequences of Poor Mental Health

- Decreased academic performance
- Decline in school attendance
- Unfavourable campus environment
- Hindered interpersonal communication
- Increased need for health services
- Missing opportunities for growth
- Poor self-confidence and self-esteem
- Untreated medical conditions
- Long-term adverse effects

## Programs & Activities

- **Introductory Session on COPE** — Counselling and Outreach for Peer Empowerment (conducted under ABET — Adithya Birla Educational Trust)
- **Spotlight Saga** — "The Perks of Being a Wallflower"
- **Gratitude Wall**
- **Menstrual Hygiene Awareness**
- Youth Mental Health First Aid Course (YMHFA)
- Webinars, workshops, and one-on-one assistance
- Games, quizzes, and open microphones
- Mindfulness workshops in dance and art therapy
- Blogs, newsletters, and articles

## Club Structure

- **4 activities each year; 1 meeting per month**
- Led by a Student Coordinator with calendar activities, newspaper, Instagram story, and Instagram page
