# Information Technology — About the Department

**Category:** Departments — IT

## Overview

The **Department of Information Technology** was launched in **2012**, focusing on comprehensive learning to benefit students in making significant contributions to the IT industry. The department has Centres of Excellence status in **Data Science**.

## Programs Offered

- **B.Tech in Information Technology** — intake: 60 (increased to 120 from 2019)
- **M.Tech in Data Sciences** — intake: 18 (from 2020)

## Key Features

- Well-equipped classrooms and computer laboratories with high-end systems, LAN & Wi-Fi
- Strong industry support and Centres of Excellence in Data Science
- Consistently impressive student placement records

## Vision

> To emerge as a center of excellence in Information Technology and to produce women technocrats, global leaders for better tomorrow.

## Mission

- **M1:** Impart quality education and inculcate problem-solving skills using latest technologies in IT
- **M2:** Encourage multidisciplinary research and consultancy projects
- **M3:** Promote industry-academia linkage and enhance entrepreneurship skills in women engineers

## Program Educational Objectives (PEOs)

- **PEO 1:** Develop strong analytical skills using fundamental concepts of science & engineering
- **PEO 2:** Excel in programming and critical thinking by applying core technical knowledge
- **PEO 3:** Exhibit continuous learning related to evolving technologies in professional career
- **PEO 4:** Demonstrate ethical behavior, teamwork & leadership qualities to solve problems in broader social context

## Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs)

- **PSO 1:** Formulate, simulate and use knowledge in domains like Computer systems, data engineering, information and network security, AI — enabling better career paths
- **PSO 2:** Provide optimized solutions using open-ended programming environment following industry practices and strategies
