## Overview

The Electrical and Electronics Engineering department lists laboratories that support practical learning in electrical machines, control systems, measurements, power systems, power electronics, circuits, simulation, projects, and electric vehicle design.

## Laboratories

- Basic Electrical Engineering Lab (BEE Lab)
- Control Systems Lab (CS Lab)
- Electrical and Electronics Design Lab (EED Lab)
- Electrical Machines-I Lab (EM Lab-I)
- Electrical Machines-II Lab (EM Lab-II)
- Power Systems Lab (PS Lab)
- Electrical Measurements and Instrumentation Lab (EM&I Lab)
- Power Electronics Lab (PE Lab)
- Electric Circuits Lab (EC Lab)
- Electrical Simulation Lab (ES Lab)
- Projects Lab
- Vishnu Vehicle Design Lab

## Basic Electrical Engineering Lab

The Basic Electrical Engineering Lab exposes students to practical execution of fundamental electrical engineering theories. It covers motors, generators and transformers in an electrically safe environment.

The lab includes energy meters, power meters, voltage meters, ammeters, transformers and motors of various sizes and types. Students learn electrical system design, network theorem analysis, and the operation of DC generators, DC motors, AC motors and transformers.

## Control Systems Lab

The Control Systems Lab is used by EEE students in the second semester of second year.

It supports hardware and simulation experiments related to feedback, PID controllers, motor characteristics, second-order system time response, and stability concepts.

## Electrical and Electronics Design Lab

The Electrical and Electronics Design Lab serves as a learning and troubleshooting platform for electrical machines, electronic circuits and switchgear.

Students learn the difference between theoretical and practical aspects, market survey of equipment, and circuit troubleshooting.

## Electrical Machines-I Lab

The Electrical Machines-I Lab provides visual and practical knowledge of DC machines. It includes DC generators, motors and rectifiers, and allows students to perform tests under different loading conditions.

## Electrical Machines-II Lab

The Electrical Machines-II Lab provides visual and practical knowledge of AC machines. It includes single-phase and three-phase transformers, induction motors and synchronous generators.

## Power Systems Lab

The Power Systems Lab supports experiments and simulation for electrical power systems. Students measure characteristics of components used in electric power engineering and study power system operation.

Equipment includes transformers, electrical machines, power passive components, power supplies, digital multimeters and laser tachometers.

## Electrical Measurements and Instrumentation Lab

The Electrical Measurements and Instrumentation Lab gives practical exposure to electrical measurement, meter standards, and instrumentation.

Equipment includes energy meters, power factor meters, AC and DC bridges, current transformers, potential transformers, strain gauges, LVDT and capacitive pick-up transducers.

## Power Electronics Lab

The Power Electronics Lab supports practical understanding of power electronic circuits and semiconductor devices.

Students work with rectifiers, AC voltage controllers, inverters, cycloconverters and choppers. The lab includes a Digital Storage Oscilloscope (DSO) and converter configurations for industrial drive applications.

## Electric Circuits Lab

The Electric Circuits Lab trains students in circuit connections, circuit-element combinations, analysis of circuits, operation of measuring devices, and estimation of electromagnetic parameters.

## Electrical Simulation Lab

The Electrical Simulation Lab develops computational and coding skills for electrical systems. Students model systems and verify performance using tools such as MATLAB and ngSpice. The lab also supports placement-oriented coding practice.

## Projects Lab

The Projects Lab enables students to develop prototypes using engineering fundamentals. It supports project expos, national and international competitions such as SIH and TIIC, conference papers, journal papers and research activities.

## Vishnu Vehicle Design Lab

The Vishnu Vehicle Design Lab provides practical training in electric and hybrid vehicle design, including E-Karts, hybrid scooters and electric bicycles.

Students work on component design, assembly, system integration, chassis, suspension, brakes, drivetrain analysis, and embedded control using Arduino and Raspberry Pi. Tools include ANSYS, MATLAB/Simulink and Fusion 360.
