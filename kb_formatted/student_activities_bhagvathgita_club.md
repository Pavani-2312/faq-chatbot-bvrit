## Overview

The Bhagvathgita Club introduces students to the Bhagavad-gītā and its philosophical context. The page describes the setting of the Bhagavad-gītā within the Mahābhārata and presents related learning resources.

## Setting of the Bhagavad-gītā

The Bhagavad-gītā appears as an episode in the Mahābhārata. It is presented as a dialogue between Lord Kṛṣṇa and Arjuna before the Kurukṣetra war between the sons of Dhṛtarāṣṭra and the Pāṇḍavas.

## Context from the Mahābhārata

- Dhṛtarāṣṭra and Pāṇḍu were brothers in the Kuru dynasty.
- Dhṛtarāṣṭra was born blind, so the throne passed to Pāṇḍu.
- After Pāṇḍu died, his sons Yudhiṣṭhira, Bhīma, Arjuna, Nakula and Sahadeva were raised under Dhṛtarāṣṭra.
- The sons of Dhṛtarāṣṭra, especially Duryodhana, opposed the Pāṇḍavas.
- Duryodhana challenged the Pāṇḍavas to a gambling match, which led to their exile.
- After exile, the Pāṇḍavas requested their kingdom, but Duryodhana refused.
- Kṛṣṇa attempted to plead for peace, but war became inevitable.
- Kṛṣṇa became Arjuna’s charioteer, which leads into the opening of the Bhagavad-gītā.
