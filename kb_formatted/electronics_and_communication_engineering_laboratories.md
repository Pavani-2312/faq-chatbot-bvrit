## Overview

The Electronics and Communication Engineering department laboratories provide hands-on experience for curriculum and beyond-curriculum practical courses. The department lists electronics, communications and simulation laboratories with hardware, software and standard operating procedures for laboratory sessions.

## Laboratories

- Electronics Lab-I
- Electronics Lab-II
- Electronics Lab-III
- Communications Lab
- Simulation Lab-I
- Simulation Lab-II

## Electronics Lab-I

Electronics Lab-I introduces undergraduate students to analog and digital electronic circuits. It develops circuit design, assembly and testing skills through experiments and IC-based applications.

**Infrastructure and capacity:**

- 12 furnished benches
- 3 students per bench
- 36 students per session
- CROs, function generators and regulated power supplies at benches

**Equipment and components:**

- Analog ICs such as op-amps and voltage regulators
- Digital ICs such as gates, flip-flops, counters, timers and encoders/decoders
- Active components including diodes, transistors and op-amps
- Passive components including resistors, capacitors and inductors
- Trainer kits, breadboards, CROs, function generators, multimeters and dual power supplies

**Labs conducted:**

- Elements of Electronics and Communication Engineering Lab
- Analog Electronic Circuits Lab
- Linear and Digital IC Applications Lab

## Electronics Lab-II

Electronics Lab-II provides practical exposure to electronic device behavior and analog circuit design. It supports experiments involving diodes, BJTs, FETs, rectifiers, amplifiers and oscillators.

**Infrastructure and capacity:**

- 12 spacious workbenches
- 3 students per bench
- 36 students per session
- CROs, function generators and regulated power supplies

**Equipment and components:**

- Diodes including Zener and PN junction diodes
- BJTs, JFETs and MOSFETs
- Resistors, capacitors and inductors
- CROs, function generators, multimeters and regulated power supplies

**Labs conducted:**

- Electronic Devices and Circuits Lab
- Analog Circuits Lab
- Electronic Circuit Analysis Lab

## Electronics Lab-III

Electronics Lab-III is a digital electronics laboratory for digital circuit design and implementation.

**Infrastructure and capacity:**

- 12 spacious workbenches
- 36 students per session
- Regulated power supplies, digital multimeters and oscilloscopes

**Equipment and components:**

- 74-series digital ICs
- Basic logic gates: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR and XNOR
- MSI ICs for multiplexers, demultiplexers, encoders, decoders, adders, subtractors and comparators
- Flip-flops, counters, shift registers and latches
- Breadboards, connecting wires and logic probe testers

**Learning outcomes:**

- Implement Boolean logic using standard digital ICs
- Design combinational and sequential circuits
- Build adders, subtractors, multiplexers, demultiplexers, encoders, decoders, flip-flop circuits, counters, shift registers and finite state machines
- Analyze and simplify logic expressions using Boolean algebra and Karnaugh maps
- Translate truth tables into circuit implementations

**Labs conducted:**

- Digital Logic Design Lab
- Digital Electronics Lab

## Communications Lab

The Communications Lab supports experiments in microwave engineering, optical communication, IoT and modulation techniques.

**Key activities:**

- Microwave and optical communication experiments, including wave propagation, guided media, antennas, waveguides, couplers and photonic systems
- Microwave power measurement and VSWR calculations
- Optical fiber transmission and reception
- Characterization of optoelectronic devices
- IoT experiments using development boards and sensors
- Cloud-based IoT applications, wireless data transmission and sensor integration
- VNA-based S-parameter measurement, impedance matching and RF component evaluation from 1 MHz to 3 GHz
- AM, FM, PM, ASK, FSK, PSK and QAM experiments

**Infrastructure and equipment:**

- Trainer kits for microwave, optical, analog modulation, digital modulation and IoT experiments
- 12 benches with 12 computers running Windows and Linux Ubuntu
- Spectrum analyzer
- 6 microwave benches

**Labs conducted:**

- Analog and Digital Communications
- Microwave and Optical Communications
- Advanced Communication Lab
- IoT Architectures and Protocols Lab

## Simulation Lab-I

Simulation Lab-I supports communications, image processing and real-time embedded systems. It enables students to simulate, analyze and implement engineering concepts using industry-relevant software tools and hardware platforms.

**Infrastructure:**

- 35 networked computer systems
- High-speed LAN and wired internet
- LCD projector
- Intel Core i3 @ 3.59 GHz processors
- 8 GB RAM
- Windows 10 / 11

**Software and tools:**

- MATLAB and Simulink campus-wide license
- Code Composer Studio
- EDA Playground
- Microwind
- QNX Software Development Platform

**Hardware development kits:**

- TMS320C6745 DSP Starter Kits

**Applications:**

- Basic Simulation Lab
- Digital Signal Processing Lab
- Signals and Systems Lab
- CMOS Analog IC Design Lab (PG)
- System Verilog Test Benches using UVM Lab (PG)
- Domain-specific trainings in communications, image processing using MATLAB, model design using Simulink and app design using MATLAB App Designer
- Real-time OS concepts in embedded systems using QNX

## Simulation Lab-II

Simulation Lab-II supports curriculum laboratories and domain-specific training in VLSI design and embedded systems.

**Infrastructure:**

- 36 computers connected through LAN and high-speed wired internet
- LCD projector
- Windows 10 / Windows 11
- Linux CentOS 7.9

**Software and tools:**

- Xilinx Vivado Design Suite for FPGA-based digital design and simulation
- Synopsys EDA tools for ASIC design flow, including synthesis, simulation, place-and-route and verification
- QNX Software Development Platform for real-time OS concepts
- NI Multisim for electronic circuit design, analysis and simulation

**Hardware development kits:**

- Nexys A7 FPGA boards
- 8086 microprocessor kits
- 8051 microcontroller kits
- ARM Cortex-M3 kits

**Applications:**

- UG Microcontrollers Lab
- UG Microprocessors and Microcontrollers Lab
- UG CMOS VLSI Design Lab
- UG Electronic Circuit Analysis Lab
- PG Digital System Design with FPGAs Lab
- PG VLSI Advanced Physical Design Lab
- Domain-specific training in VLSI design flow using Synopsys tools
- Domain-specific training in real-time embedded systems using QNX
