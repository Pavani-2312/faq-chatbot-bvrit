Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) Committee
The Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) Committee is established to oversee and manage matters related to intellectual property within the institution. Its primary goal is to promote innovation while safeguarding the rights of creators and inventors.
Responsibilities
Policy Development
: Create and update policies related to intellectual property management and protection.
Review and Assessment
: Evaluate and assess new inventions, research outcomes, and creative works for potential intellectual property protection.
Support and Guidance
: Offer support to researchers and creators in navigating the process of securing intellectual property rights.
The IPR Committee is dedicated to promoting a culture of innovation while ensuring that the rights of creators are protected. By fostering an understanding of intellectual property, the committee aims to enhance the institution’s contribution to research and development.
